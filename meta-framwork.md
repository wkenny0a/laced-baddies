# Meta Ad Creative Framework

Use this file as the standing framework for Laced Baddies Meta-related work, including Instagram/Facebook ad concepts, carousel ideas, Reels scripts, comment-to-DM flows, offer testing, landing page prompts, and image/video creative prompts.

When generating Meta ad ideas, do not start with generic demographics or generic wig features. Start with the buyer's exact beauty situation, the emotional buying trigger, the objection, and the moment she is trying to create.

## Project Read

Laced Baddies sells a glueless wig experience called The Crown. The current storefront positions it as a fast, protective, confidence-first wig for buyers who want to look installed without glue, scissors, salon time, or stress.

### Primary Persona

The 30-Second Crown Buyer

She wants the confidence of a fresh install but does not always have the time, money, patience, or edge health for a salon appointment. She may already wear wigs, protective styles, or lace installs, but she is tired of glue mess, lifting lace, late-night maintenance, and wigs that look good only when a stylist touches them.

Her internal line:

"I want to look done without doing the most."

### Primary Angle

The no-glue, no-stress crown that gives her a fresh look in seconds.

This angle should sell the transformation first:

- Before: late, tired, edges stressed, wig not laid, no salon appointment.
- After: slip it on, adjust the band, walk out looking polished.

### Primary Offer

30-Day Baddie Club

A fresh glueless wig delivered every month, with the tools that make the experience feel complete.

Use the 30-day Baddie Club as the hero offer:

- $99 per month
- Billed $99 monthly
- Fresh wig every 30 days
- Includes the Free Welcome Kit: bald cap, silk sleep mask, wig storage bag, and spray comb
- Future rotation gifts include the melting spray duo in month 3 and silk bonnet in month 6
- Cancel or pause anytime
- 30-Day Baddie Quality Guarantee

Use the one-time purchase as the comparison anchor:

- $130 once for the 26-inch crown
- Wig plus free wig storage bag
- No subscription
- No full Welcome Kit or future rotation gifts included

## Core Principle

Strong Meta ads are built from four parts:

1. A hyper-specific beauty situation
2. A sharp selling angle
3. A value-framed offer
4. A hook that makes the viewer feel, "This is for me"

For Laced Baddies, the creative should usually sell the moment before it sells the wig:

- Waking up late but still looking put together
- Going out without booking a stylist
- Protecting edges without giving up a laid look
- Getting a fresh crown before the old one feels tired
- Opening the box and seeing the full routine ready to go
- Leaving the house with a look that feels effortless

## Instructions For Generating Personas

To generate successful personas, move beyond broad demographic labels. Personas should be archetypes based on specific problems, desires, and buying triggers.

### Avoid Broad Demographics

Weak personas:

- Women who wear wigs
- Beauty shoppers
- Black women
- Busy professionals
- People who like hair products
- Women aged 18-45

These are too vague and lead to forgettable creative.

### Build Hyper-Specific Archetypes

Strong personas describe the exact scenario the buyer is in.

Examples:

- A protective-style girl who wants a break from glue because her edges need recovery, but she still wants to look fully done.
- A buyer who wakes up late for work or class and needs a wig she can put on without a full install routine.
- A wig wearer who loves lace looks but hates trimming lace, melting spray buildup, and guessing whether the wig will stay secure.
- A beauty buyer who wants a fresh rotation every few months so she does not keep stretching an old wig past its prime.
- A going-out buyer who wants a quick backup look for last-minute plans, dates, dinners, trips, and weekends.
- A first-time wig buyer who is scared of glue, scissors, and complicated tutorials, but still wants a natural-looking result.
- A budget-conscious baddie who likes the salon look but does not want every style change to become a $200 appointment.
- A subscription buyer who wants beauty restocked automatically because she already knows she will need another wig later.

### Identify Granular Pain Points

Drill into root causes and real anxieties.

For Laced Baddies, segment broad wig buyers into specific problems:

- "I want a wig I can actually put on by myself."
- "I do not want to damage my edges just to look cute."
- "I hate when lace starts lifting before I even leave the house."
- "I do not have time to glue, cut, melt, style, and pray."
- "I need a backup look for days when my hair is not done."
- "I want a fresh wig before my old one starts looking tired."
- "I am scared a cheap wig will look fake in person."
- "I want the install look without sitting in a chair for hours."
- "I want the whole kit, not just the wig."
- "I need something secure enough for real life, not just a video."

### Specify Nuances

For each persona, define:

- Their specific pain point
- The language they use
- The emotional trigger
- The type of proof they need
- The offer that would move them
- The creative format that fits their buying stage

Example:

Persona:
The Edge-Recovery Baddie

Pain:
She loves laid installs but knows glue and repeated tension are stressing her edges.

Language:
"I need to leave my edges alone."
"I still want my hair to look done."
"I do not want to fight with glue anymore."

Proof needed:
Close-up hairline shots, dome cap fit demonstration, before/after install clips, real customer reviews, wear-test footage, clear explanation of how it stays on.

Buying trigger:
Seeing a model slip the wig on and look finished without glue.

Best offer:
The 90-Day VIP Box with the wig plus styling kit, framed as a low-stress crown rotation.

## Instructions For Generating Angles

An angle is the specific argument or perspective used to sell the solution to the persona's problem. Do not simply list product features.

A strong angle should blend several of the following tactics.

### Problem Agitation

Lead with the pain point so the viewer feels understood before introducing the product.

Examples:

- "If your lace only looks good after a full routine, this is the wig you keep on standby."
- "Glue should not be the reason you feel pretty leaving the house."
- "Stop fighting with lace when you already have somewhere to be."
- "Your edges deserve a break, but your confidence does not have to."
- "That old wig you keep reviving? Put it in retirement."

### Contrarian Truth

Challenge a common belief to create cognitive friction.

Examples:

- "A good wig should not require a stylist every time."
- "The easiest install is the one you do not have to install."
- "Fresh hair should not have to mean glue, scissors, and a three-hour routine."
- "Your everyday wig should work on real mornings, not just content days."
- "A beauty subscription makes sense when the product is something you actually replace."

### Social Proof And Authority

Use proof that matches the persona.

For Laced Baddies, useful proof includes:

- Real customer try-on videos
- Close-up hairline footage
- Side-by-side before/after clips
- Wear-test clips
- Packaging unboxing
- Reviews about speed, comfort, security, and edge protection
- Founder story or Black-owned brand message
- Clear subscription value comparison

### Curiosity Gap

Open with an information gap that makes the viewer want the reveal.

Examples:

- "The wig I keep in my car for emergency hair days..."
- "The no-glue crown I wish I had before my edges needed a break..."
- "Three reasons this became my weekday wig..."
- "What comes in the Laced Baddies VIP Box?"
- "The 30-second hair routine I use when I refuse to be late."

### Comparison And Objection Handling

Acknowledge what the buyer has likely tried or feared.

Examples:

- "No glue. No lace cutting. No salon appointment."
- "The wig for buyers who want the install look without the install stress."
- "A fresh wig rotation without guessing when to reorder."
- "Not a one-time cheap wig you forget about. A crown routine."
- "The kit is for the days when you need the whole look handled."

### Transformation

Frame the angle around before/after.

Examples:

- Before: bonnet, late morning, no time. After: crown on, outfit ready, out the door.
- Before: glue buildup and edge stress. After: glueless fit and a softer routine.
- Before: old wig losing shape. After: fresh box arrives before the look gets tired.
- Before: random wig and separate tools. After: wig, box, spray, brush, mirror in one system.
- Before: nervous first-time wig buyer. After: slip-on confidence without scissors.

## Instructions For Generating Offers

The offer has two parts:

1. The product itself
2. The value proposition, meaning how the value is framed

If the core product cannot change, create new ways to package and frame the value.

### Value Proposition Framing

Test offers such as:

- 90-Day VIP Crown Rotation
- 30-Day Baddie Club
- Glueless starter kit
- Edge-break beauty bundle
- Emergency hair day kit
- Fresh crown subscription
- First-time wig buyer kit
- Black-owned beauty box
- Wig plus care essentials
- Limited-time free styling kit
- Free 3-day shipping
- Subscribe and save compared with buying once
- Gift-ready crown box

### Avoid Defaulting To Deep Discounts

Do not rely only on heavy discounts. Deep discounts can lower perceived value and train shoppers to wait for sales.

Prefer value-building offers first:

- Full kit included
- Fresh wig rotation
- Subscription savings
- Free shipping
- Protective, glueless routine
- Easy first-time setup
- Secure dome cap fit
- Black-owned brand story

Use discounts strategically, especially for retargeting, urgency, or comment-to-DM flows.

### Match Value To Persona

The offer must match the buyer.

Examples:

- Edge-recovery buyers: glueless routine, secure fit, edge protection proof
- Busy buyers: 30-second routine, no salon, no glue, no scissors
- Subscription buyers: fresh crown rotation, automatic delivery, kit included
- First-time wig buyers: beginner-friendly setup, step-by-step demo, no cutting
- Going-out buyers: emergency hair day kit, ready-in-seconds proof
- Budget-conscious buyers: salon-look confidence, lower monthly entry price, bundle value

## Instructions For Generating Hooks

A hook is not a clickbait trick. It is a promise that the ad is highly relevant to the viewer.

The first 3 seconds must make a stranger understand what the ad is about.

### Hook Criteria

Evaluate hooks based on:

- Clarity: Is it instantly understandable?
- Relevance: Does the buyer feel seen?
- Novelty: Does it feel different from competitor ads?
- Specificity: Does it include concrete detail?
- Credibility: Does it show proof, real product, real outcome, or authority?

Avoid directly calling out generic personas.

Weak:
"Wig lovers, listen up."

Stronger:
"Your edges deserve a break, but your hair can still look done."

### Proven Hook Frameworks

#### The Contrarian Truth

Formula:
Everything you have been told about [industry/topic] is wrong.

Laced Baddies examples:

- "Everything you have been told about lace installs is too much work."
- "The easiest install is the one you do not have to install."
- "A fresh wig should not require glue, scissors, and a stylist."

#### Specific Proof

Formula:
I achieved [desired outcome] in [timeframe] without [common sacrifice].

Examples:

- "I got a fresh look in 30 seconds without glue."
- "I left the house looking installed without cutting lace."
- "I changed my whole look before work without booking a stylist."

#### The Truth Bomb

Lead with an uncomfortable truth.

Examples:

- "You are not lazy. Some wigs are doing too much."
- "If your wig only works after a full routine, it is not your everyday wig."
- "A tired wig can make the whole outfit feel unfinished."

#### Psychological Confrontation

Use a direct, pattern-interrupting statement.

Examples:

- "Stop making glue part of your morning routine."
- "Stop reviving the wig you already know is tired."
- "Stop waiting on a stylist just to feel like yourself."

#### Founder Or Brand Letter

Build tension and personal stakes.

Examples:

- "We built Laced Baddies for the girls who want the crown without the glue struggle."
- "We were tired of wigs that looked easy online and complicated in real life."
- "This is for the buyer who wants beauty to feel ready, not stressful."

#### Sensory / ASMR Hooks

Use 2-3 seconds of extreme close-ups.

Visual ideas:

- Box opening
- Hair moving through the brush
- Spray mist hitting the hairline
- Adjustable band tightening
- Lace close-up against skin
- Mirror snap open
- Wig placed inside the storage box
- Model flipping hair after slipping it on

## Laced Baddies Meta Creative Rules

### Always Show The Ease

The strongest creative shows the buyer the process:

- Open the box
- Take out the crown
- Slip it on
- Adjust the band
- Smooth the hairline
- Brush through
- Check the mirror
- Walk out

Do not only say:
"Glueless wig."

Say:
"The wig you slip on when you have 30 seconds and still need to look done."

### Sell The Moment

Do not only say:
"26-inch HD lace wig."

Say:
"The crown you put on when your hair is not done but your plans are."

Do not only say:
"Includes brush and mirror."

Say:
"The whole kit for emergency hair days, fresh wig days, and out-the-door mornings."

### Use Comment-To-DM For Fit And Confidence Questions

Strong comment CTAs:

- "Comment CROWN and I will send the VIP Box details."
- "Comment GLUELESS and I will send the 30-second routine."
- "Comment EDGES if you want the no-glue breakdown."

DM flow:

1. Ask whether she wants one-time, 30-day, or 90-day.
2. Ask what matters most: speed, edge protection, beginner-friendly setup, or fresh rotation.
3. Send the matching product explanation and link.
4. Address fit, security, and what comes in the box.
5. Add urgency or bonus if one is active.
6. Follow up within the allowed messaging window.

### Best Offer Language

Prefer:

- No glue, no scissors, no salon stress
- Fresh crown rotation
- The VIP Box includes the wig and styling essentials
- Your emergency hair day kit
- Built for real mornings
- Gives your edges a break
- Slip on, adjust, and go
- Black-owned beauty brand

Avoid:

- Overly technical wig construction language without showing the result
- Generic "shop now" without a reason
- Cheap-sounding bargain language that weakens the premium feel
- Talking only about inches, lace, and cap details without the buyer's situation

## Creative Formats To Test

### UGC Try-On Reel

Best for:
Cold traffic and first-time buyers.

Structure:

1. Open with the problem moment.
2. Show the wig going on.
3. Show the finished look.
4. Explain no glue, no scissors, no stylist.
5. Show the VIP Box.
6. End with the subscription or one-time offer.

### Emergency Hair Day Reel

Best for:
Busy buyers and going-out buyers.

Structure:

1. Start with "My hair was not done and I had to leave in 10 minutes."
2. Show bonnet or undone-hair moment.
3. Slip on the wig.
4. Adjust band and brush through.
5. Final outfit reveal.
6. CTA to get The Crown.

### Edge Break Carousel

Best for:
Buyers who already understand wig installs.

Slides:

1. "Your edges deserve a break."
2. "No glue."
3. "No lace cutting."
4. "Secure dome cap fit."
5. "Fresh crown in seconds."
6. "Choose one-time, 30-day, or 90-day."

### VIP Box Unboxing

Best for:
Subscription buyers and value framing.

Structure:

1. Show the closed branded box.
2. Reveal the wig.
3. Reveal the storage box.
4. Reveal spray, brush, and mirror.
5. Show finished look.
6. Compare VIP Box with one-time wig-only option.

### Founder/Brand Story

Best for:
Trust building and retargeting.

Structure:

1. Black-owned brand message.
2. Why glue-heavy routines are frustrating.
3. Who The Crown was built for.
4. Show product and kit.
5. Invite the buyer into the rotation.

## Meta Concept Output Format

When generating Meta concepts, use this structure:

### Concept Name

Short title.

### Persona

Specific archetype, not a broad demographic.

### Pain Point

The exact frustration or desire.

### Angle

The selling perspective.

### Hook

First 3 seconds.

### Visual Direction

What appears on screen.

### Script / Caption

Ad copy or video script.

### Offer

How the value is framed.

### CTA

Comment, click, shop, subscribe, or DM.

### Landing Page

Where the user goes and why.

### Proof Needed

Reviews, try-on footage, hairline close-up, wear test, unboxing, founder proof, shipping proof, etc.

## Landing Page Alignment

Every ad should land on a page that repeats the same promise.

If the ad says:
"Fresh look in 30 seconds."

The landing page should immediately show:

- A model wearing the wig
- The no-glue promise
- The plan options
- The kit contents
- The simple 1-2-3 process

If the ad says:
"Your edges deserve a break."

The landing page should immediately show:

- Glueless construction
- Secure fit explanation
- Customer proof or close-up footage

If the ad says:
"VIP Crown Rotation."

The landing page should immediately show:

- 90-day plan savings
- What comes in the box
- How billing works
- How it compares with the one-time purchase

## Quality Bar

Before using any Meta concept, ask:

- Is this specific enough that the buyer feels seen?
- Is the hook clear in 3 seconds?
- Does it sell a moment, not just the wig?
- Does it show or imply ease?
- Does it make the offer feel valuable without sounding cheap?
- Does the CTA tell the buyer exactly what to do?
- Does the landing page match the ad promise?
- Is there enough proof to reduce hesitation?

If the answer is no, rewrite the concept.
